Mission

SUST Cybersecurity Club focuses on learning and spreading knowledge of different aspect of cyber security.

It is runned by a group of students who have strong enthusiasms in computer, network, hardware, software, mobile and internet of things (IoT) security.

The purpose of this organization is to facilitate students’ cybersecurity research and practice, learn about security tools and protocols, penetration testing and especially to prepare students for the cybersecurity competitions such Capture The Flag (CTF), and others.

This is a community for aspiring security enthusiasts, and for those wanting to understand the realm of information security to apply in information technology, day to day life and business process



Who can join?

Anyone studying or studied in SUST.

Topics of Interest Include:

    Penetration Testing -- hacking of computer security systems through technical means to determine how secure they are (with permission, of course)

    Malware and Reverse Engineering -- Information on current malware security threats, notable past ones, how they are created and function, and how to detect and protect from them.

    Cybersecurity Software and Hardware -- Software and hardware tools used by administrators to safeguard systems, as well as those used by malicious agents to access protected systems.

    Cryptography -- How encryption and decryption of information works, methods used by malicious agents to bypass or break encryption, and methods used to encrypt data for malicious reasons.

    Social Engineering -- Methods used by those with malicious intent to access computer systems by means of social manipulation, personal information collecting, and other means.

    Offensive (Red Team) Tactics -- Vulnerabilities used to access systems and data such as phishing, backdoors and bugs, as well as activities committed with that access, such as DDoS attacks, botnets, covert cryptocurrency mining, doxxing, etc.

    Defensive (Blue Team) Tactics -- Methods used to protect the personal information, data, and identity of security conscious individuals, including those used by malicious actors, such as VPN's, Tor, 2FA, etc. Use of IPS, IDS, SIEM, SOAR etc.

    Blockchain -- How blockchain works, idea generation and project development.
